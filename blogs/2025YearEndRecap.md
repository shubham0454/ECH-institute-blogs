### Women in Ethereum Protocol (WiEP) Study Groups

2025 is the second year that ECH Institute is supporting WiEP. Each year, there are two study group cohorts that run over a ten-week period, led by our community contributors. Each cohort attracts a large number of women developers who are interested in core protocol development. Our study groups in 2025 are organized and coordinated by [Katya](https://x.com/Sib_Katya) (Cohort 3), [Mercy](https://x.com/naps_thelma) and [Arunima]() (Cohort 4). ### EIP Office Hours and Governance Meetings

To support EIP and ERC authors move along their proposals from draft to review and to last call, ECH has been organising weekly EIP Office Hours with EIP/ERC editors. Community members can also participate in the governance calls that take place every month, known as the EIP Improvement Process (EIPIP) meetings.

We hosted **34** EIP (and ERC) Office Hours and **10** EIPIP meetings this year! ðŸ”¥ We are grateful for our EIP Editors who provided invaluable support, [lightclient](https://x.com/lightclients), [Sam](https://x.com/_SamWilsn_), [Victor](https://x.com/ZainanZhou), [Gajinder](https://x.com/g11tech) and our newest edition to the team, [Jochem](https://x.com/JochemBrouwer96). 

### Protocol calls streaming

Each week, there are multiple core protocol calls happening on Ethereum where core developers and protocol builders discuss important impact and consequences of Ethereum Improvement Proposals. We help publicise these calls by re-streaming them on Twitter (X) and also uploading them on Spotify.

### First-ever Twitter spaces celebrating International Women's Day!

![X](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ethcatherders/ech-blog/refs/heads/main/blogs/images/IWDCeleb.png)

We spotlighted women builders, entrepreneurs and key ecosystem players to celebrate International Women's Day! Joining us for the big day were [Nixo](https://x.com/nixorokish) (Protocol Coordinator at Ethereum Foundation), [Katya](https://x.com/Sib_Katya) (Core protocol developer), [Sejal](https://x.com/sejal_rekhan)(ex Ecosystem Growth, Allo Capital), [Yoyo](https://x.com/yoyocao_eth) (Co-initiator, ETHPanda), [Laura](https://x.com/laurabxyz)(Co-founder, Jobstash), [Alisha](https://x.com/futurealisha) (Co-founder, FireflyPocket) and [Ornella](https://x.com/OrnellaWeb3) (Community, Ethereum Foundation).

Each of them shared incredible insights into the challenges and opportunities growing Ethereum communities, from onboarding new builders into the ecosystem, to producing open-source hardware and software (upholding trustless, decentralized ethos of Ethereum) and encouraging more builders to contribute to Ethereum's core protocol infrastructures.

Our speakers also highlighted the realities of working in a remote-first environment, the trade-offs experienced as women and mothers in this space.
